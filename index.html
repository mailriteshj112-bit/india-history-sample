The Indus Valley Civilization

The Indus Valley Civilisation (IVC), also known as the Harappan Civilisation, was a Bronze Age civilisation in the northwestern regions of South Asia, lasting from 3300 BCE to 1300 BCE.

Figure 1: Artifacts and Maps of the region.

Key Achievements

  • Urban Planning and Drainage Systems
  • Standardized Weights and Measures
  • Metallurgy (Copper, Bronze, Lead, Tin)
